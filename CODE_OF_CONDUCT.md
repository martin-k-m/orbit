# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want Orbit to be a project where anyone can contribute without harassment,
regardless of experience, background, identity or how they show up. We pledge to
make participation a respectful experience for everyone.

This project follows the spirit of the
[Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)
(v2.1). If you have read that, you already know what to expect here.

## Our standards

Behaviour that builds a good community:

- Being kind, welcoming and patient — especially with newcomers.
- Assuming good faith and asking questions before assuming malice.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback on the **work**.
- Taking responsibility, apologising to those affected, and learning from it.
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its users.

Behaviour that is not acceptable:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discriminatory jokes and language.
- Sexualised language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Personal or political attacks, insults, or derogatory comments.
- Publishing others' private information (physical or email address, etc.)
  without explicit permission.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issues or reviews.
- Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions, commit
messages, code review — and when representing the project in public.

## Enforcement

Report unacceptable behaviour privately to the maintainers via
[GitHub](https://github.com/martin-k-m). Reports will be reviewed promptly and
treated confidentially. We will not name reporters without their consent.

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards and may take any action
they deem appropriate, including editing or removing comments, commits, code and
issues, or temporarily/permanently banning contributors for behaviour they
consider inappropriate.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may
face repercussions determined by other project leaders.
